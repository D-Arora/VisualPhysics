VISUAL PHYSICS ONLINE

DOING PRACTICAL WORK IN THE 21th CENTURY

NOTES FOR TEACHERS

 

We need to do away with some of the traditions of the 19th and 20th centuries and move into the 21st century when doing practical activities in physics.

 

         NO PAPER: We no longer need paper based notes on experiments. Less paper means more efficiency and less costs. As a teacher, you do not have to spend your valuable time photocopying and distributing the notes on an experiment. My notes on practical activities are formatted to be viewed on a mobile phone, tablet computer or computer. They are not necessarily print friendly. The vast majority of students have a mobile phone and they can download (usually pdf format) the practical activity and read it before class. They can access all the information electronically. Students dont end up with lots of sheets of paper but have easy access to all practical activity documents.

 

       LOGBOOKS: It is advisable that all students have a logbook which is an A4 bound book preferably each page with a grid to make it easy to draw graphs (no need for graph paper). Real scientists dont write aim, method, theory, results etc for their experiments but rather that keep a detailed logbook of their experimental work. So, this is what we should be doing in class, not writing up experiments in the traditional manner but learning how to effectively record practical activities in a logbook.

 

 

LOGBOOK

 

1.                     Start each new practical activity of a new page.

2.                     Number each page.

3.                     Write the date on the top of each page.

4.                     Write the name of the practical activity of the new page.

5.                     Write a short introduction about the practical activity.

6.                     Record all your measurements in your logbook (check: symbols used, significant figures, units, tables).

7.                     Add any appropriate diagrams.

8.                     Add any appropriate comments.

9.                     Draw scientific graphs.

10.               Answers all questions (for example use headings such as Logbook 6).

11.               At the end of the practical activity, write a short review.

12.               Use MS EXCEL (spreadsheets) for data recording, graphs, data analysis.

 

       TEAM WORK: Do practical activities in teams of three students. Three is better than two as it encourages greater communication about the physics. Also, the team of three can keep one logbook. In many ways, this is better than a single logbook for each student, since students need to engage with each other on the recording in the logbook. Students if they want their own copy can use their mobile phone.

 

       REFLECTION: At the end of the practical class or the next class have a short reflective discussion about the activity. Physics Education research has shown that reflection periods can have a dramatic effect upon improving the value of practical work in the class room.
